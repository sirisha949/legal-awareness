Right to Information (RTI)

The Right to Information (RTI) is an act of the Parliament of India which sets out the rules and procedures regarding citizens' right to information. Under the provisions of the RTI Act, any citizen of India may request information from a public authority, which is required to reply expeditiously or within thirty days.

Obtaining Information: Citizens can file RTI requests to obtain information from any public authority.
Transparency and Accountability: RTI helps in making the functioning of government departments transparent and holds them accountable.
Decision-Making: Information obtained through RTI can help in informed decision-making and advocacy.

Example 1: A citizen files an RTI request to obtain information about the funds allocated for a local road repair project.
Example 2: An NGO uses RTI to gather data on the implementation of a government welfare scheme in a specific region.
Example 3: Journalists use RTI to uncover details about government contracts and expenditures.

Promote Transparency: Ensure that the functioning of government bodies is transparent.
Accountability: Hold public authorities accountable for their actions.
Empower Citizens: Empower citizens with the information necessary to participate actively in democracy.

Step 1: Identify the Department: Determine which public authority holds the information you need.
Step 2: Write the Application: Write an application addressed to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the concerned department.
Step 3: Pay the Fee: Pay the prescribed fee (usually a nominal amount).
Step 4: Submit the Application: Submit the application via post or in person.
Step 5: Await Response: The PIO must respond within 30 days. If the information is not provided, you can file an appeal.
